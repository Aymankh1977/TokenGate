- Check their latest news and announcements
- Identify their target customers
- Note their competitive positioning

**Step 2: Customize the Subject Line**
- Reference their recent announcement (if applicable)
- Mention specific models or features
- Create urgency or curiosity

- Tailor the opportunity to their business model

## TIPS FOR SUCCESS

1. **Personalization is Key**
   - Generic emails get deleted
   - Reference specific models, features, or news
   - Show you've done research

2. **Lead with Value**
   - Don't ask for partnership, offer it
   - Show what's in it for them
   - Quantify the opportunity (revenue, reach, etc.)

3. **Keep It Short**
   - Busy executives skim emails
   - Get to the point quickly
   - Use bullet points and short paragraphs

4. **Make It Easy to Say Yes**
   - Suggest specific meeting times
   - Provide multiple contact options
   - Remove friction from the process

5. **Follow Up Persistently**
   - Don't give up after one email
   - Follow up 2-3 times before moving on
   - Try alternative contacts if available

6. **Use Social Proof**
   - Mention other providers you're integrating with
   - Reference traction metrics
   - Show momentum

7. **Be Authentic**
   - Write like a real person, not a bot
   - Share genuine enthusiasm
   - Be honest about your business

## COMMON OBJECTIONS & RESPONSES

**Objection 1: "We already have direct sales channels"**

Response: "That's great! TokenGateway isn't a replacement—it's an additional 
distribution channel. You'll reach developers who prefer aggregated platforms 
and don't want to manage multiple vendor relationships. Plus, you get revenue 
with zero sales/marketing costs."

**Objection 2: "We're concerned about brand dilution"**

Response: "We prominently feature your branding and models on TokenGateway. 
Developers see your name and logo on every interaction. You're not losing 
brand presence—you're gaining new brand exposure."

**Objection 3: "What about revenue share? Can we negotiate?"**

Response: "Absolutely. The 85/15 split is our standard starting point, but 
we're open to negotiation based on volume, exclusivity, or other factors. 
Let's discuss what works best for both parties."

**Objection 4: "How do we know this will generate significant revenue?"**

Response: "Great question. We're projecting [X] developers in Year 1, with 
average monthly spend of [Y]. That translates to [Z] annual revenue for you. 
We can also include a minimum revenue guarantee if you want certainty."

**Objection 5: "We need to review this with our legal team"**

Response: "Absolutely. I've attached our standard partnership agreement. 
Your legal team can review it, and we can discuss any changes. I'm happy 
to work with your team to address any concerns."
